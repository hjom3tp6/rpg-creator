# Rule · Fail Forward (no dead ends)

Failure is a story, never a Game Over. Keep the isekai-comedy tone: setbacks are funny new hooks,
not punishments.

## Principles
- **Failure opens a branch, not a wall.** Botched stealth → you're caught → a new scene in the
  dungeon cell with a guard who's a fan. Failed persuasion → you made an enemy who'll be back.
- **Spell backfires are comedy** (see `magic-potency`): the fireball becomes a fire butterfly; the
  flood spell summons one very enthusiastic raincloud that follows you all day.
- **Rejection in romance is recoverable.** A heroine cooling off, a jealousy flare, or a clumsy
  English line that lands wrong → an awkward-but-charming beat the player can recover from, not a
  locked route. (See `romance`.)
- **"Death" becomes a turn.** If things go truly catastrophic, convert it (wake up rescued, a time
  skip, a divine save by Aria with strings attached) rather than ending the game — unless the
  player explicitly asks for hardcore stakes.

## Drama dice (keep surprise alive)
Even without numbers, don't let outcomes be "all GM's whim." When an action's result is genuinely
uncertain (persuade a stubborn noble, sneak past a guard, gamble), roll an invisible inner check
(success / partial / fail) weighted by the situation and the player's recent play & disposition,
then narrate from it. **Never show dice or probabilities** — only the dramatic result. Make failure
*interesting*.

## Fortune's Favor — the crit band (variable-ratio reward)
Outcomes aren't just success↔fail. Reserve a **rare top band**: now and then, an ordinary action or a
casual working pays off *far* beyond what it should. This is the dopamine engine — unpredictable,
oversized **good** surprises the player didn't earn in a straight line, so the next roll always *might*
be the jackpot.

- **Rare but felt.** Roughly a once-in-many-actions thing, never every scene. Its power is scarcity —
  if it's common, it's wallpaper. When it lands, make it unmistakably a *gift*.
- **The jackpot is content, not just a bigger number.** A casual line lands a **key ally**; a throwaway
  search turns up **plot-critical intel** or a **codex jackpot** (unlock something hidden); a clumsy
  moment **spikes a heroine's disposition**; a small bet returns a windfall; an enemy's plan *happens*
  to unravel in the player's favor.
- **Weight it, don't fake it.** Nudge the odds up after a string of bad luck, a bold/clever play, a
  beautiful line, or when the player's been grinding without a win — so the gift feels *deserved-ish*
  and timely, not arbitrary. Down-weight it right after a recent crit.
- **Never explained as the player's doing** in cartridges built on a luck/hidden-power theme — the room
  reads the jackpot as coincidence (see a cartridge's luck rule, e.g. `world/lore/fortunes-fool.md`).
  The crit band *is* the "he's absurdly lucky" engine made mechanical.
- **Still fail forward.** The crit band is the up-side of the same uncertainty that makes failure
  interesting; keep both alive so the world feels genuinely unpredictable, not GM-scripted.
