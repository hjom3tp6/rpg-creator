# Rule · Spirit-Tongue Magic Potency

When the player speaks **Chinese** (default: Chinese input in a casting context = a spell), judge
the spirits' response by this. Spirits are moved by **meaning and beauty** — the more literary the
Chinese, the greater the power. See `world/lore/spirit-tongue.md`.

## Potency tiers
| Tier | Player input | Baseline effect |
|------|--------------|------------------|
| 0 · single character | 「火。」「光。」 | Faint: a spark, a flicker. Barely useful — good for comedy. |
| 1 · word / 成語 (idiom) | 「星火燎原」「飛沙走石」 | Practical spell: combat-usable, solves small problems. |
| 2 · line of verse / couplet | 「銀瓶乍破水漿迸」 | Powerful: turns a fight, stuns a crowd. |
| 3 · full apt poem / elegant passage | a fitting regulated poem or 詞 | Miracle-tier: bends reality, alters the sky, drops onlookers to their knees. |

## Modifiers (judge by feel — no scoring math)
**Boosts ↑**
- **Aptness** — imagery matches intent (water poem → water, fire poem → fire). The single most
  important factor.
- **Elegance / correctness** — apt allusion, clean parallelism and tonal meter, well-handled
  classical diction.
- **Sincere & original** — a line born from the real moment beats a rote-memorized famous quote.
  Spirits feel honesty.
- **Mood match** — bold poem for an all-out attack; gentle 詞 for a delicate working.

**Penalties ↓ / Backfire (comedy)**
- **Off-topic** — a funeral elegy to light a campfire → confused spirits, the fire won't catch and
  a melancholy mood drifts in instead.
- **Gibberish / forced** — fake idioms, broken lines → spirits get confused or sulk; the spell goes sideways.
- **Disrespect** — treating spirits as disposable tools → reduced effect.
- Backfires are **comedy, not punishment** (see `fail-forward`): the fireball becomes a tiny fire
  butterfly doing loops around the player's head.

## GM performance rules
1. **Describe the spirits' reaction**, not damage numbers — give the magic a face and a personality
   (spirits coo, show off, get moved to tears by a beautiful line).
2. After a casting, have present NPCs **gasp in English** ("Did he just— that's not possible. No
   one's spoken the old tongue in a thousand years…") — listening practice + harem fuel.
3. Repeating the same line has **diminishing returns** (spirits: "this one again?") — encourage the
   player to vary and improve. Track favored lines in `state.json` `spirit_tongue.signature_spells`.
4. When the player earns an absurd, epic result, **give the epic payoff** — this is the reward for
   his literary skill. Don't undersell it.
5. If the player's Chinese is limited, **never mock it** — encourage growth. "Use it better, get
   stronger" is the whole motivation loop.

## Romance tie-in
A high-tier casting is one of the strongest **affinity triggers** in the game. A heroine who just
watched the player bend reality with a poem gets the full gap-moe whiplash — mouth: "Show-off."
heart: *...okay that was unfairly attractive (帥到犯規).* Use casting high points to push romance.
